5 Low-Maintenance Plants That Thrive in Arizona Heat

Not all plants can take the desert sun—but these can! Succulents like agave and echeveria thrive in full sun with minimal watering, storing moisture in their thick leaves to survive the hottest months. Lantana, with its bright, multi-colored blooms, and desert marigold, known for its golden flowers and resilience, are both colorful additions that attract pollinators without demanding much care. And let’s not forget rosemary—a fragrant, evergreen shrub that’s as tough as it is beautiful.

These plants don’t just survive in the Arizona climate—they flourish in it. Whether you’re planting a new bed or refreshing a tired space, these choices bring lasting beauty with little effort. Perfect for busy homeowners or those new to gardening, they keep your yard vibrant and drought-tolerant year-round.
